The maker

Dave Kube

I spent about thirty years building homes across northeast Nebraska. That work was good — I built things that stand — but it takes a toll on your body. A few years back I started spending more and more time in the shop making furniture, and I realized I liked it a lot more than framing walls.

These days I build finished pieces on my own schedule and sell them when they're ready. Every piece I make is something I'd put in my own house: solid wood, honest joinery, built to last longer than I will. I lean toward heirloom furniture — dining tables, benches, end tables, cabinets — but I also enjoy smaller work like cutting boards, boxes, and one-off pieces that strike my interest.

I'm based in Crofton, Nebraska, out near Lewis & Clark Lake. I work primarily in the hardwoods that grow here and grow well — black walnut, hard maple, white oak, cherry — because I like the idea of Nebraska wood ending up in Nebraska homes.

How it works

I make pieces, then I sell them. This is not a commission shop — I don't build to order. What you see on this site is what exists or what I'm currently building. If you're interested in something you don't see here, reach out; I'm happy to talk about whether something similar is in the works or could be.

All sales are local pickup only. I don't ship furniture. If you're in the region and want to come out and see a piece in person before committing, that's how I'd do it too.
